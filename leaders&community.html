Leaders & Community

The Muslim community in Rwanda is guided by respected religious leaders and scholars who work to promote Islamic knowledge, unity, and social development. These leaders organize religious activities, provide guidance, and represent the community in national and international matters.

Current Grand Mufti

Sheikh Musa Sindayigaya

Elected in May 2024 as the head of the Rwanda Muslim Community (RMC). He is responsible for leading Islamic affairs, promoting unity, and strengthening Islamic education and community development programs.

Former Mufti

Sheikh Salim Hitimana

Contributed significantly to the growth of the community by helping expand Islamic institutions, including mosques and schools across the country.

Scholars, Imams & Teachers

Leadership in Rwanda also includes scholars and imams who guide communities in mosques and schools. They teach the Qur’an and moral values while playing a vital role in charity work and interfaith dialogue. Through their guidance, the community continues to promote peace, cooperation, and national unity.
